About Halloween

Halloween, celebrated on October 31st, is a time for costumes, candy, and spooky fun. The holiday's origins trace back to the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain, a time when people believed the boundary between the worlds of the living and the dead was blurred.

During Samhain, people lit bonfires and wore costumes to ward off roaming ghosts. As the centuries passed, the celebration evolved, blending with Roman traditions and later Christian practices. By the 19th century, Halloween had become a community-centered holiday with parades, games, and the now-familiar tradition of trick-or-treating.

In modern times, Halloween is a celebration of all things spooky and fun. People decorate their homes with jack-o'-lanterns, haunted houses, and cobwebs. Costumes range from the frightening to the whimsical, with parties and gatherings held in the spirit of fun and fright.
